What this page is

This is the rights and catalog layer of Texas Movement. It shows how Distribution protects catalogs, runs platforms, and connects to HERO and the wider system.

Who it’s for

Brand owners and partners with real catalog and IP exposure—products, media, and identity that appear across marketplaces and social surfaces and need to stay accurate and authorized.

Teams that are tired of playing defense reactively and want a documented, repeatable operation instead of a scramble every time something breaks.

It is not for anyone seeking legal advice or a one-click fix. Enforcement here is evidence-based and takes discipline.

How it works

Rights first. The catalog is grounded in clear rights records before anything touches a platform.

Documented chain. Every action—from listing changes to enforcement—is documented so it holds up when challenged.

Platform-native. Operations run in the surfaces where your catalog lives, not in abstract spreadsheets.

Enforce calmly. Infringement is handled through a repeatable workflow, backed by records, not panic.

Services

IP defense operations

We monitor the surfaces where your brand appears, document infringement and misuse, and act through a repeatable takedown workflow. The result is enforcement that is calm, consistent, and backed by records.

Catalog protection

We keep listings accurate, authorized, and consistent across platforms and prevent unauthorized or duplicate entries from degrading the catalog.

Platform listing management

We operate storefronts and listings across surfaces with correct data, media, and rights so your catalog stays legible to customers and partners.

Rights records

We maintain the documentation that establishes ownership and supports enforcement when your catalog is challenged or misused.
